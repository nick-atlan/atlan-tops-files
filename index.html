What this means

! BH-active interviewers take a median 26.7h to submit vs 13.8h for those not using it. The autofill integration isn’t reducing friction yet — likely because the Ashby scorecard forms aren’t structured correctly for it to work.
! 6 high-volume BH-active interviewers are below 55% completion. They have recordings in BrightHire but aren’t using autofill to close the loop in Ashby. This is a behavior gap, not a tool awareness gap.
+ Jack Caine: 38 interviews, 94.7% completion, 1h 17m avg — without BrightHire. Sets the benchmark the BH-active cohort should be hitting once the scorecard forms are fixed and autofill is working properly.

Next steps

1 Fix Ashby scorecard forms — prerequisite for autofill to work. Until forms are structured correctly, nudging people to use BrightHire autofill creates frustration, not habit. (Nick + Natalia — in progress.)
2 Update the Ashby invite template — add a single line instructing interviewers to enable BrightHire before joining. Zero ongoing cost, reaches 100% of interviewers at the point of scheduling. (Ashby — one-time change.)
3 Update the 1-hour Slack reminder — add a prompt to launch BrightHire before the call and use autofill after. Fires at the highest-intent moment, already wired into Ashby. (Ashby — one-time change.)
4 Direct outreach to flagged interviewers — Vartika, Manu, Shouryadeep, Suhas, Gopal, and Lane are recording but not closing the loop in Ashby. Personal nudge from their recruiter, not a broadcast. (TA directors — this week.)
5 Post-interview autofill nudge — Slack DM 2 hours after interview ends if feedback not yet submitted, with a direct link to the Ashby scorecard. Fires once per interview, suppressed if feedback already in. Sits alongside Ashby’s default reminder but is BrightHire-specific. (n8n or Zapier — build after scorecard forms are fixed.)
6 Re-run this analysis in 60 days — once scorecard forms are fixed and steps 2–5 are live, this is when you can make a fair assessment of whether autofill is reducing time to submit and moving completion rates.
